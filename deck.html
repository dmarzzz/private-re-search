private research log

private-re-search

A corpus and five reports on anonymous search: how you query without revealing who you are, and how that changes when the searcher is an agent.

01 · the question

One thread, followed all the way down

It started with a private repo and a single question: how do you search the web without handing over your IP or letting your queries be linked to you.

That pulled in adjacent questions from there. Why Tor-routed search gets blocked. What the residential-proxy market really is. And what the search problem even is, formally, once an agent is the one searching.

02 · method

Two engines, different jobs

research-swarm

Your rotate research agent. Runs on a separate key, so it works in parallel without competing for budget. It gathers links and archives the raw page contents into the corpus.

claude workflows

Multi-agent fan-out: parallel lenses, then adversarial verification of every claim and citation, then synthesis. This does the gap-finding and the reports.

gather (swarm)+ verify + synthesize (claude)→ corpus + reports

05 · the proxy question

Does a residential proxy keep you private?

Three friends, three prompts: SearXNG over Tor got blocked everywhere, industrial scraping buys thousands of residential IPs, and some of those sit in China.

The answer: no. A residential proxy is a trust-shift, not a privacy gain. You hide your IP from the destination and hand one commercial intermediary full, billing-linked visibility, with none of the cryptographic unlinkability that Tor, OHTTP, or PIR provide.

06 · the formalization

What the search problem actually is

Three traditions that rarely cite each other: AI state-space search, decision-theoretic search with optimal stopping, and information-retrieval ranking. We showed they are three projections of one frame.

J(π, σ) = E[ U(bτ, T) − Σ c ]

Expected terminal value minus accumulated search cost. 71 verified references.

07 · human vs agent

Same problem, different searcher

human

Cost is attention and time. Answers from snippets and scent. Few queries. Stops when it feels good enough.

agent

Cost is tokens, compute, latency. Reads full text. Many parallel queries. Stops on a budget or a confidence gate.

Who is searching becomes a parameter of one problem, not a choice between two unrelated ones.

08 · the two follow-ons

Closing the open threads

  • When to stop. metareasoning
    The stopping rule as a value-of-computation threshold. Where clean optimal-stopping rules exist, and why they break for a retrieval agent.
  • Private search for agents. the latency flip
    Privacy becomes a term in the objective. Mixnets and PIR that are unusable for impatient humans become viable defaults for a patient agent.
