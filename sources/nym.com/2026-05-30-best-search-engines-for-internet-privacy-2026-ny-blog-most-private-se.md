# The most private search engines in 2026: Search without being tracked

How to compare features, block trackers, and search without being followed


[Casey Ford. PhD](https://nym.com/blog/author/casey-ford)Technical reviewer

###### Share

Search engines are where we begin almost every online journey. But they’re also one of the biggest privacy risks. If you're using a mainstream engine like Google, you're giving away a detailed map of your interests, habits, and even your location to a Big Tech data collectors out there.

If you want to break free from this surveillance model, **private search engines** offer a safer path. This article explores the **best private search engines available in 2026** — and how pairing one with [NymVPN](https://nym.com) gives you complete control over your digital footprint.

## What makes a search engine "private" exactly?

Private search engines are designed to give you results without collecting your personal data. But privacy isn't a single switch: it’s a combination of technical and policy choices. Here's what the most private search engines usually have in common:

**No logging:**They don’t store your IP address, search queries, or browser fingerprint**Open-source code:**Some engines allow anyone to inspect how they work**No third-party ads:**This eliminates scripts and pixels which can be used to track your activitySome engines allow you to run your own version[Decentralized](https://nym.com/blog/what-is-decentralization)or self-hosted options:**Jurisdictional advantage:**Servers located in countries with strong digital privacy protections

If your search engine doesn’t meet these standards, it’s likely leaking a lot more data than you think.

Want to know what really exposes your identity online? It’s not just cookies: it’s [metadata](https://nym.com/blog/what-is-metadata). That includes your IP address, time of connection, and device fingerprint, all of which can be logged even if the search engine says it doesn’t track you.

## Comparing the most private search engines

To help you choose the best option, here’s a breakdown of top private search engines, how they store data, and where they’re hosted.

Each of these search engines has strengths and trade-offs. For example, [Startpage](https://www.startpage.com) provides Google-level results without trackers, while [YaCy](https://yacy.net) is fully decentralized. So choose based on how much control and anonymity you need.

## Can you trust private search engines?

Using a private search engine is a big privacy win — but it doesn’t make you invisible. Most private engines don’t track your searches, but they also don’t mask your IP, protect you from [DNS leaks](https://nym.com/blog/dns-leaks), or hide your traffic metadata.

That’s why pairing a search engine with a privacy-first VPN is essential. It’s the difference between clearing your tracks and never leaving any to begin with.

## Open source vs. closed source: Why it matters

Transparency is a key part of trust in any privacy tool. **Open-source search engines** allow anyone to inspect the code, identify vulnerabilities, and verify claims about how data is handled.

**Closed-source** alternatives, by contrast, require blind trust: you can’t see how the code handles your data behind the scenes. For those who want full transparency, open-source engines like [SearXNG](https://searx.space/) and [YaCy](https://yacy.net/) stand out to the Nym team.

For more on why this matters, read Nym’s guide [What is open source?](https://nym.com/blog/what-is-open-source)
