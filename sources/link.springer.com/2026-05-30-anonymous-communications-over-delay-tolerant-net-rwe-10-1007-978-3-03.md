## Definition

The use of opportunistic store-carry-and-forward networks to provide anonymous communications among the participating nodes. The objectives are to (i) hide the identity of the sender from the receiver and/or (ii) hide the identities of the communicating nodes from an adversary.

## Background

Anonymous communications are vital in many critical applications, including resistance against oppressive governments and state-sponsored surveillance or whistleblowing. For example, a whistleblower may want to communicate with a journalist without revealing his/her identity, which could compromise their safety. On the other hand, oppressive governments may want to identify all individuals that are involved in any type of communication with known government critics because it is very likely that they too share similar anti-government sentiments.
